Definitions

Explanatory Notes for Completing Questionnaire

Date Established: This refers to the date that the institution first opened its doors for admission of students.

Education District:The grouping of two or more schools according to the proximity in location or communities located.

Age: refers to the age of person as of October 15th of the current year.

School Enrolment Capacity: This refers to the number of students that the original school building plus any subsequent additions, was built to accommodate.

New Entrants: Fresh intake into kindergarten. Note this does not refer to students on transfer to your school.

Graduates: Graduates are students who have successfully completed Grade Six.

Leavers: This refers to students who have completed primary school and have not gained entrance to a secondary school.

Residential Communities:Only major communities should be listed.

Active PTA: This refers to a PTA which meets at least once a term.

Graduate Teacher: A Graduate Teacher is a one with at least a Bachelors degree.

Trained teacher: A Trained teacher is one who has successfully completed a recognized course of training in teacher education from an accredited institution.

Non Graduate:A Non Graduate is a teacher without at least a Bachelors degree.

Graduate trained teacher (GT):Graduate trained teacher (GT) is a teacher with at least a Bachelors degree that has formal and recognised training in teacher education from an accredited institution.

Non Graduate Trained Teacher: Non Graduate Trained Teacher: is a teacher without a degree but is trained.

Teachers on Study Leave: These are teachers who are still on staff but not in the classroom. They have been granted study leave (with or without pay).

Continuous Professional Development (CPD):A structured approach to learning to help ensure competence in practice, taking in knowledge, skills and practical experience. CPD can involve any relevant learning activity, whether formal and structured, non-formal, informal and self-directed.

Basic Computing Skills: includes an understanding of the basic notion of the computers, manipulation; managing files, word processing, spread sheets, databases, presentations, researching online, and communicating with others using the computer. International Standard Classification of Education,2011

Dropout: a student who leaves school for any reason before graduation or completion

Government Assisted: an education institution which is partially supported by the Government

Hall-type: the class is organised in an open space separated by the chalk board

Repeater:Pupil enrolled in the same grade for a second or further year

Improved water sources: include piped supply, tubewell/borehole, protected well/spring, rainwater catchment, and packaged or delivered water. Unimproved sources include unprotected well/spring, and surface water (e.g. lake, river, stream)

Improved sanitationincludes flush/pour-flush toilets, pit latrines with a slab or platform, and composting toilets. Unimproved facilities include pit latrines without a slab or platform, hanging latrines (hole over water), and bucket latrines.
