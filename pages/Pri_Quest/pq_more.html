A Graduate Teacher is a one with at least a Bachelors degree.
A trained teacher is one who has successfully completed a recognized course of training in teacher education from an accredited institution.
A Non Graduate is a teacher without at least a Bachelors degree.
Graduate trained teacher (GT) is a teacher with at least a Bachelors degree that has formal and recognised training in teacher education from an accredited institution.
Non Graduate Trained Teacher: is a teacher without a degree but is trained.
Teachers on Study Leave: These are teachers who are still on staff but not in the classroom. They have been granted study leave (with or without pay).
Continuous Professional Development (CPD): A structured approach to learning to help ensure competence in practice, taking in knowledge, skills and practical experience. CPD can involve any relevant learning activity, whether formal and structured, non-formal, informal and self-directed.
Basic Computing Skills: includes an understanding of the basic notion of the computers, manipulation; managing files, word processing, spread sheets, databases, presentations, researching online, and communicating with others using the computer. Improved water sources include piped supply, tubewell/borehole, protected well/spring, rainwater catchment, and packaged or delivered water. Unimproved sources include unprotected well/spring, and surface water (e.g. lake, river, stream).
